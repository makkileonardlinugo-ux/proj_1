Why This Planet

The Case for Kepler-452b

Solar Analog Host

Kepler-452 is a G2V star, nearly identical in mass, temperature, and luminosity to our own Sun. It is 6 billion years old, 1.5 billion years older than Earth's star, making this system the most mature habitable-zone analog in the catalog.

Star Class G2V (Sun Twin)
Orbital Sweet Spot

At 1.046 AU, Kepler-452b orbits almost exactly where Earth orbits our own Sun, but around a slightly older, slightly brighter star. The result is a 384.8-day year that nearly mirrors our own, inside a confirmed habitable zone.

Orbital Distance 1.046 AU
Super-Earth Scale

At 1.63 times Earth's radius and five times its mass, Kepler-452b has 2.7 times Earth's surface area and likely stronger geological activity. More world means more opportunity, whether for settlement, extraction, or long-term terraforming.
